# Classic Dutch Stamppot: Boerenkool met Worst

Stamppot is the quintessential Dutch winter dish. This version — boerenkool met worst — combines curly kale mashed with potatoes and is served with a smoked sausage called rookworst, traditionally made by Unox or Hema.

## Ingredients (serves 4)

- 1 kg floury potatoes (e.g. Bildtstar or Eigenheimer variety)
- 500 g curly kale (boerenkool), shredded
- 2 smoked sausages (rookworst), approx. 300 g total
- 100 ml whole milk
- 50 g unsalted butter
- Salt, pepper, and freshly grated nutmeg

## Method

1. Peel and roughly cube the potatoes. Boil in salted water for 15 minutes.
2. Add the kale to the boiling potatoes for the final 5 minutes.
3. Meanwhile, heat the rookworst in simmering water — do not boil — for 10 minutes.
4. Drain the potatoes and kale. Mash together with warm milk and butter.
5. Season generously with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
6. Serve with sliced rookworst on top and a dash of mustard on the side.

> **Tip:** In the Netherlands, stamppot season traditionally runs from October to March. Albert Heijn and Jumbo supermarkets both sell pre-shredded boerenkool during these months.
