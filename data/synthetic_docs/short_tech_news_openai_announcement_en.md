# OpenAI Unveils GPT-5 at San Francisco Developer Conference

**San Francisco, CA** — OpenAI CEO Sam Altman took the stage at the Moscone Center on March 15, 2025, to announce the release of GPT-5, the company's most powerful language model to date.

The event, attended by over 3,000 developers and investors, showcased GPT-5's improved reasoning capabilities and dramatically reduced hallucination rates. Altman claimed the model scores 94% on the MMLU benchmark, surpassing Google DeepMind's Gemini Ultra 2.0, which scored 91% on the same evaluation.

"This is the model we have always dreamed of building," Altman told the crowd. "GPT-5 doesn't just answer questions — it reasons, plans, and corrects itself in real time."

Microsoft, OpenAI's largest investor with a $13 billion stake, confirmed that GPT-5 will be integrated into Azure AI Studio and Microsoft Copilot by April 2025. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella joined via video call from Redmond, Washington, to formally endorse the partnership.

Critics, including AI safety researcher Geoffrey Hinton, a former Google engineer and Nobel Prize laureate, called for independent safety evaluations before widespread commercial deployment. The EU AI Act, which came into full effect in January 2025, mandates risk assessments for all general-purpose AI systems deployed in the European Union.
