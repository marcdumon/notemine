# Ajax Stun Bayern Munich in Champions League Quarter-Final First Leg

**Amsterdam** — Ajax produced a stunning upset at the Johan Cruyff Arena on Tuesday evening, defeating Bayern Munich 2–1 in the first leg of their UEFA Champions League quarter-final.

Goals from Brian Brobbey (32') and Devyne Rensch (67') gave Ajax a commanding lead, before Harry Kane pulled one back for the German champions in injury time. The result sends Ajax into the second leg at the Allianz Arena in Munich with a precious away advantage.

Ajax head coach Francesco Farioli praised his team's tactical discipline. "We worked the entire week on exploiting their high defensive line," the Italian coach said in his post-match press conference. "The players executed perfectly."

Brian Brobbey, 24, now has nine Champions League goals this season, the most of any player in the competition. Bayern Munich manager Vincent Kompany admitted his side were "too open in the second half."

The second leg is scheduled for April 15 at the Allianz Arena in Munich. Ajax last reached the Champions League semi-finals in 2019, when they were eliminated by Tottenham Hotspur in dramatic circumstances.

KNVB chairman Just Spee called the result "a historic night for Dutch football."
